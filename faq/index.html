FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about working with Priorify.

Is a PriorityPath® project a consultation?

No — it's a facilitation. The PriorityPath process is fully dependent on insights collected from your own stakeholders. Priorify facilitators NEVER insert their own opinions, experiences, or ideas. We organize and illuminate what your people already know.

How is this different from a survey or engagement platform?

Surveys capture surface-level responses with low trust. PriorityPath uses confidential 1-on-1 interviews by a neutral third party, where anonymity promotes genuine candor. The data is organized into cause-and-effect relationships and mathematically prioritized — not just averaged.

What kind of problems can PriorityPath solve?

Our process is industry and problem agnostic. Past applications include strategic planning, restructuring, M&A integration, manufacturing improvement, safety management, resource allocation, product launches, and conflict resolution — across four continents.

What does the free Priorify Pulse® tier include?

Pulse provides an instant AI-powered analysis including a priority map, hidden insight analysis, and a downloadable summary. Based only on your input (no stakeholder interviews) — powerful for initial framing, but limited compared to multi-stakeholder intelligence.

Who should use Priorify Pro® vs. PriorityPath?

Pro works well for operational prioritization, product strategy, and departmental alignment where you can survey stakeholders directly. For politically sensitive issues where anonymity, trust, and facilitated group alignment are critical, PriorityPath with analyst-led interviews is recommended.

How long does a full PriorityPath project take?

Typically 3–5 weeks. Compare this to 26+ weeks at major consulting firms. AI tools accelerate every step while analysts ensure interviews, validation, and facilitation maintain the depth that drives real alignment.

Has the technology been independently validated?

Yes — by the U.S. Army TRADOC, ExxonMobil, Stanford Research Institute, the U.S. Veterans Administration, and the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Agency. It was found to be the only method that mathematically considers interactions among all issues simultaneously.

How is AI used in the process?

AI enhances every step: pre-engagement research, interview transcription and synthesis, diagram auto-generation, adaptive rating interfaces, scenario simulation, group disagreement analysis, and ongoing progress tracking. But human interviews, facilitated discussions, and trust-building remain human-led.

What industries do you serve?

All of them. We've completed projects in aerospace, defense, energy, healthcare, technology, manufacturing, government (US, Canada, NZ, Australia, UK), utilities, and more. The process is agnostic because it's driven by your stakeholders, not our industry expertise.
